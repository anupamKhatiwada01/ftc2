No Gym, No Problem: How to Stay Motivated with Home Workouts

With the world being such a busy place, and time being something we have less of every day, home workouts are becoming more and more popular

One of the hard things about this style of training, that many people seem to struggle with, is keeping motivation high

Here’s 8 tips I found to stay motivated and keep your momentum going at home

Tip #1 - Set Clear Goals
#

When you know where you’re going, it’s easier to get there

With clear goals in mind, you can keep chipping away at them, until you reach where you want to be

Setting a goal like “Get fit” won’t cut it

You have to make them S.M.A.R.T.

S. Specific - Like I said before, “Get fit” won’t cut it. Make it more along the lines of “train 3 times a week to lose weight, build muscle, and see my abs”

M. Measurable - Can you measure it? Ask yourself “Can I see my abs?” No? You still have work to do

A. Attainable - It has to be something you can actually work towards. Being able to do no handed pushups for example, isn’t a thing, so don’t even go there.

R. Realistic - it’s no good saying “by tomorrow” because that won’t happen. You also can’t work towards becoming bulletproof - just keep it real

T. Time based - give yourself a realistic deadline. Even if you don’t quite reach your goal in that time frame, you’ll be a lot better than when you started

Personally, I work in 90 day sprints, then evaluate the goal

Now that you know about SMART goals, a good example would be “train 3x a week for the next 90 days so I can see my abs”

That’s actually doable and will keep you on track

Tip #2 - Create a dedicated space
#

Having a dedicated space really helps with motovation

You have a set area to go to when it’s time to train, kind of like going to a gym

You have everything you need right there in one place, so there’s no time wasting fetching equipment from around the house

This makes the perceived effort needed to get a training session in, much lower in your mind

You don’t need to take up an entire room either

My space takes up a doorframe and an exercise mat

You could throw in some parralettes and dip bars which could be put to one side when not being used

Whatever you have though , make sure you have easy access to it from where you train

Tip #3 - Follow a routine
#

It’s far easier to stay consistent when you have a set routine to follow

Take all the guess work out of it and it becomes a habit a lot easier

You know on Monday - you do a b c, Tuesday - X y z, and so on for the whole week

Once it becomes a habit, the motivation to train becomes irrelevant

You just do it

Try to stick to working out at the same time of day and treat each session as an important appointment you have with yourself

You can’t let yourself down this way

I have a rule I stick to for this:

Never miss a Monday

The rest of the week just falls into place after a Monday session

Tip #4 - Train with friends
#

Having a training buddy puts pressure on you to show up

You can’t let your friends down now can you?

Sometimes this can be a challenge when training at home, so joining an online community is a great idea to get around this

When you surround yourself with other people on a similar path, you enjoy talking about progress, programs and seeing what others are doing

Tip #5 - Use Technology
#

Apps on your phone make tracking workouts easier

Some also have communities built in

They can have structured programs for you to follow or make tracking your own a piece of low calorie cake

I use Notion for tracking progress and X for the community side of fitness

Fitness X is full of great people so come and say hello some time

Tip #6 - Mix things up
#

Mixing things up is a cheat code for staying motivated

Boredom provides resistance which leads to quitting

We definitely don’t want that

Although I recommend sticking to a proven program for building muscle, and burning fat, rest days are a different kettle of fish

You could go for a long walk, go swimming, take a hike up a mountain, do some jump rope, or play on the beach with some friends

Keeping things fun keeps them interesting and enjoyable

Tip #7 - Reward yourself
#

Rewarding yourself improves motivation on a psychological level

When you have to go shopping for new clothes because your old ones are too big, it feels great

A day at the spa for hitting a target is another great reward

Even a movie night with the family and some popcorn is a great way to reward yourself

When you have something to look forward to on top of your goals, it makes them more enjoyable

Being able to tell yourself “I earned this” gives you a great sense of accomplishment

Set up a reward system for yourself and keep the gains coming

Tip #8 - Stay Positive
#

This gets easier the more you train

In the beginning though, keeping a positive mental attitude will keep you going

Focus on the benefits you’re getting until you feel them kicking in

The mental health benefits of this alone make it worth while

Life happens at times, and a positive outlook will keep you on the rails

Setbacks don’t mean failure

Staying set back does

Positivity and focusing on the benefits is the key to keep you on your journey, and reach your goals

Bonus tip - Forget Motivation
#

Motivation just gets you started

It could be an event that happens or something somebody says

That one event isn’t enough to keep you going in the long run

You need discipline

Discipline is when you do the work, even when you don’t feel like it

You’re never going to think “man, I regret that workout”

I guarentee you think “man I regret not working out” though

Sometimes it’s unavoidable, but if you can do the work, you should do the work, no matter how you feel about it, and you know this

We all get days when our minds will tell us not to bother that day, but you must

Even if you just do one set of pushups, you’ll find that once you start, you’ll complete your workout for the day

Conclusion
#

So there’s my tips about staying motivated and a bonus that overrides them all

  1. Set S. M. A. R. T Goals
  2. Create a dedicated space
  3. Follow a routine
  4. Train with friends
  5. Use Technology
  6. Mix things up
  7. Reward Yourself
  8. Stay Positive
  9. Use Discipline

Try to remember: little by little, it becomes a lot

One day you’ll look back on your progress and think “wow, I did all that” and feel proud of yourself

I promise

You just have to keep pushing forwards
